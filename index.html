How it all started

The COVID-19 pandemic has been raging across the country over the last year, and has only gotten worse with this wave. As a consequence it left the country in a state of disarray, people were unable to gain access to scarce resources like oxygen, medication, beds, food and tiffin services, ambulance services, teleconsultation and the like. Directing people towards verified resources was the need of the hour, the driving force behind our initiative, Tavissi Jain, took it upon herself to bridge this gap in information. She played a pivotal role in helping direct people to oxygen cylinders in Delhi, where she served as the Oxygen Support Coordinator for the Miranda House Covid Help Desk in New Delhi. After watching Coimbatore succumb to the second wave of the pandemic, she decided to put her experience to good use by starting the Coimbatore Covid Aid and directing the citizens in and around Coimbatore to quintessential resources. She realised it wasn't enough and decided to recruit student volunteers to her cause and expand the reach of Coimbatore Covid Aid in order to better aid fellow citizens. Our group has been working tirelessly in order to keep up with the increasing demand in oxygen cylinders, attributed to the expeditious rise in COVID-19 cases. Born out of this need was our “Help Tiruppur Breathe” project that aims to raise funds in order to procure oxygen cylinders. The Tiruppur District administration has graciously agreed to take up the responsibility of refilling the cylinders on rotation and their subsequent delivery to hospitals, this will ensure a smooth supply. Another initiative that we have up and running is our “Food Chapter” that is geared towards delivering connect families that are willing to share food with those that are quarantined due to covid. We also realised the disadvantages of the digital divide between a metropolitan cities and relatively smaller cities and decided to work on breaking down crucial covid related information that is verified by a team of doctors, we publish this information on all our social media platforms in Tamil as well as English to ensure accessibility to all. To learn more about us and to stay updated, follow us on social media.
